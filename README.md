\# Social Engineering Attacks Research Report



\# Overview

This repository contains a detailed cybersecurity research report on social engineering attacks created as part of a Security Analyst Internship program.



The report focuses on how cybercriminals exploit human psychology through techniques such as phishing, spear phishing, pretexting, baiting, quid pro quo, and tailgating to gain unauthorized access to systems and sensitive information.



\# Objective



The objective of this project is to:

\- Research different types of social engineering attacks

\- Analyze real-world cyberattack case studies

\- Understand the impact on organizations and individuals

\- Explore modern prevention and security awareness techniques



\# Topics Covered



\- Introduction to Social Engineering

\- Phishing Attacks

\- Spear Phishing

\- Pretexting

\- Baiting

\- Quid Pro Quo

\- Tailgating

\- Business Email Compromise (BEC)

\- Real-World Cybersecurity Incidents

\- Organizational \& Individual Impact

\- Prevention Techniques

\- Security Awareness Best Practices
